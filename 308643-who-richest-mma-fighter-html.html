Who is the richest MMA fighter in 2022? The list of top 20

MMA (mixed martial arts), also known as no holds barred, or ultimate fighting, is a full-combat sport that is primarily based on ground fighting, grappling, and striking. The sport incorporates numerous techniques from other combat sports such as kickboxing, boxing, and wide-ranging martial arts from all over the world. Some household names in MMA have amassed fortunes from their exploits in and out of the ring. Who is the richest MMA fighter, and how much are they worth?

Typically, MMA fighters make money competing in matches. However, most of the richest ones have additional incomes that include endorsements, investments, and sponsorships.

Urijah is an American mixed martial artist renowned for his impressive career in the WEC (World Extreme Cage-fighting) and the UFC. He won the WEC Featherweight Championship in 2006 and has competed for the Bantamweight Championship four times.

Urijah is credited with popularizing the UFC's lightweight classes. Before him, fans of the UFC mainly focused on Middleweight and Heavyweight divisions. Urijah retired in 2017 but made a brief return to the UFC in 2019. In 2017, he was inducted into the UFC Hall of Fame.

19. Michael Bisping: $9 Million

Michael Bisping is a former British UFC fighter who works as a sports commentator and analyst. He has also landed a few acting roles in the recent past. During his years as a mixed martial artist, Bisping competed in the UFC's Light Heavyweight and Middleweight divisions, eventually winning the Middleweight Championship.

He also participated in the now-defunct Cage Rage's Light Heavyweight Championship. Bisping is credited with being the first British fighter to compete in the UFC and the first one to win a championship when he did so at UFC 199. Since then, no other British fighter has won a UFC Championship.

18. José Aldo: $9 Million

Jose Aldo is a Brazilian mixed martial artist renowned for his several championships in the UFC and WEC. Before the WEC and UFC merged, Jose had won a WEC Championship and three UFC Featherweight Championships. In 2021, he became one of the top bantamweight fighters in the UFC competition.

Up until 2005, Aldo was undefeated in his MMA career. His first loss came against Luciano Azevedo. Still, he went on another 18-match unbeaten streak that Conor McGregor eventually stopped in 2015. Aldo is considered one of the greatest MMA featherweights of all time.

15. Junior dos Santos: $10 Million

Junior dos Santos is a Brazilian mixed martial artist born in Cacador, Santa Catarina. He began studying Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu in high school. However, at the time, he was studying to improve his overall health and not necessarily become a professional fighter in the UFC. His trainer, Yuri Carlton, saw some potential in the young Junior dos Santos and invited him to train in the mixed martial arts.

Junior began fighting professionally in 2006 after about a year of MMA training. He won 6 of his first 7 bouts and quickly gained the attention of numerous stakeholders in the UFC. He then joined the Heavyweight division in 2008 and went on to win 15 of his 16 matches, 11 of them through knockouts. In May 2012, he was the reigning UFC Heavyweight Champion.

14. Chael Sonne: $10 Million

Chael is a retired American UFC fighter whose career began in 1997 and ended in 2014. Shortly after joining the UFC, Chael rose through the ranks in the competition's middleweight and lightweight divisions. Besides the UFC, Chael also competed in Bellator MMA, Pacrase, and the WEC.

11. Frank Mir: $11 Million

Frank is also the only fighter to ever win a UFC match through a toe hold submission. Some of his most memorable victories have been against Brock Lesnar and Tank Abbott. He was the first opponent to knock out Antonia Rodrigo and force him into submission.

In the past, MMA fighters' primary source of income was the after-match salaries and payments. However, in recent years, the competitions' structure has evolved significantly, giving athletes additional avenues to make more money.

How much do MMA fighters make? Those with a massive global following on social media often attract high profile brands and sponsors, resulting in better pay per match. UFC salaries are based on low, middle, and top tiers. Most new fighters are in the low tier and typically earn anything between $10,000 and $30,000.

Mid-tier fighters include those who have already won several bouts and have begun making a name for themselves in the MMA world. Depending on one's popularity, these ones get paid anything between $80,000 and $250,000 per match.

Top-tier fighters have millions of fans across the globe and are viewed by companies as excellent avenues for marketing and promotion. These include the likes of Conor McGregor. Fighters in this category can earn anything from $500,000 to millions of dollars per fight.
