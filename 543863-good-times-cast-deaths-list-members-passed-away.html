Good Times cast deaths: List of all members who have passed away

The cast of the classic sitcom Good Times has been a significant part of television history. The show aired from 1974 to 1979 and showcased the African-American experience through humour and love. Unfortunately, many decades after the show, some cast members have passed away. So, which are the Good Times cast deaths?

TABLE OF CONTENTS

Good Times is a classic American sitcom that originally aired from 1974 to 1979 on the CBS television network. The show is set in Chicago and follows the life of the Evans family, an African-American family living in a public housing project.

The series is known for its unique blend of humour and social commentary, addressing issues such as poverty, racism, and the struggle for upward mobility. During its six-season run, Good Times was a cultural phenomenon.

Is Thelma from Good Times still alive?

Bern Nadette Stanis, who played Thelma Evans on Good Times, is among the Good Times cast still alive. She has continued to act and appear in various projects, including a regular role as Nee Nee Duncan in The Family Business: New Orleans.

Is Michael from Good Times dead?

Ralph Carter, who played Michael Evans on Good Times, is alive. However, he has not appeared in many projects. He last appeared in the 2024 film CHAAW: Chapter 3 (Christmas time is here), portraying Pastor Evans.

Did the cast of Good Times get along?

While the Evans family on Good Times appeared as a loving family on television screens across America, some cast members had different relationships off the screen.

Jimmie Walker, who played J.J. Evans, stated that he and his fellow cast members did not have a close relationship while filming the show. He mentioned that he did not remember speaking to Esther Rolle, who played Florida Evans and thought the same about John Amos, who played James Evans.

Walker also spoke about Amos' exit from the show and what he thought was the cause, stating that Amos was a good actor but that the show was against anything and everything he believed in.
